Scenario text:
Angie feels like everyone she meets is a new friend. She wants her circle to be wide. She does not need a lot of intimacy or frequency for those relationships to be important to her. It’s most important to her to be liked and to be part of whatever is going on at the time. Angie attends events and goes out with groups of friends on a regular basis. She rarely has a personal visit with just her and a friend.

Update on Angie

As Angie gets older, friends have moved away or stopped getting out as much. Some have passed away. She retired last year and only keeps up with a handful of co-workers. She used to have three or four visits with groups of friends a week. Now, she goes out less than once a week. She frequently says she is bored and wishes she had something to do. Staff help her look through her contact book and decide who to call. This was a strategy that used to work for Angie. But now, it doesn’t often lead to Angie getting together with friends.
