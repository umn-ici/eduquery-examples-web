Aerobic or Endurance Activities

These kinds of activities increase a person’s breathing and heart rate. They help to keep a person’s heart and lungs healthy. A person does aerobic activities for a long period of time. For example, running, swimming laps, or a dance class are all examples of aerobic activities. You might see an area in a gym or fitness center called ‘Cardio.’ This would include those machines or activities that would be used for aerobic exercise.

Muscle Strengthening Activities

These kinds of activities work the body’s muscles and help to make them stronger. They are also called “strength-training” or “resistance training” exercises. Exercise activities that work the muscles usually use weights or elastic (resistance) bands. Some activities include weight-lifting, push-ups, and pull-ups.

Bone Strengthening Activities

These kinds of activities help to improve bone strength or support bone growth. Bone strengthening exercises can overlap with other types of exercise. For example, bone strengthening activities can include running, fast walking, or weight-lifting.

Balance and Flexibility Activities

Balance activities help to prevent falls, whereas flexibility activities help to stretch a person’s muscles. These types of activities help a person move around better and more safely. Yoga is an activity that includes both balance and flexibility exercises.
